How to Increase Oxygen Levels in Your Body Naturally

Why Oxygen Levels Matter More Than You Think

Every cell runs on oxygen. The brain consumes 20% of your oxygen supply despite being 2% of body weight. When oxygen delivery is suboptimal — due to shallow breathing, poor posture or sedentary lifestyle — every system suffers. You feel fatigued, foggy and physically weak. Increasing cellular oxygen is one of the fastest ways to elevate every aspect of health and performance.

Kapalabhati: The Oxygen Maximiser

Kapalabhati — Skull Shining Breath — is the most powerful technique for increasing oxygen. Take a natural inhale. Then perform a sharp, forceful exhale through the nose, driven by sudden abdominal contraction. Allow the inhale to happen passively. Repeat at one exhale per second for 20-30 repetitions. This forcefully expels CO2 and creates space for oxygen-rich air to fill the lungs completely.

The 4-7-8 Oxygen Optimiser

This technique maximises alveolar gas exchange — the process by which oxygen enters the blood. Inhale for 4 counts. Hold for 7 counts (allowing full oxygen absorption across the lung membrane). Exhale for 8 counts (complete CO2 elimination). The extended hold dramatically increases the partial pressure of oxygen in the lungs, driving more oxygen into the bloodstream.

OM Chanting and Oxygenation

OM chanting requires and trains deep, controlled breathing. The extended exhale of each OM — sometimes lasting 15-20 seconds — is a powerful lung strengthening exercise. Over time, OM practice increases lung capacity and respiratory efficiency, resulting in higher baseline oxygen saturation throughout the day.

Signs Your Oxygen Levels Are Improving

Sharper mental clarity (brain receives more oxygen). Increased physical energy throughout the day. Improved sleep quality and depth. Faster post-exercise recovery. A general sense of vitality and aliveness. Your cells are waking up — and with consistent practice, they stay awake.
